# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Household/family members sharing one recipe collection. Accounts have a role (member/admin) and each recipe carries a public/private flag, so sharing within the group is a deliberate choice, not just a permissions afterthought. The dominant session is short and goal-driven: someone is in or near the kitchen, deciding what to cook, and needs the right recipe's ingredients and steps in front of them with minimal friction.

## Product Purpose

A shared household recipe box: capture, organize, and quickly find recipes to actually cook from. Success is opening the app and landing on the right recipe fast, with ingredients and steps laid out for real-time cooking use, not just browsing or admiring.

## Brand Personality

Tidy & editorial. Calm, curated, magazine-like personal cookbook feel — confident structure and generous whitespace, with the recipe content itself as the visual focus. Not a cold/clinical admin tool, not a loud or gamified consumer app.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboard density (data tables, KPI cards, sidebar-heavy chrome) — this is a cookbook, not an analytics tool.
- Recipe-blog clutter — ads, life-story preambles, popups — the opposite of "find it fast."
- Playful/gamified consumer-app styling (badges, confetti, mascots) — too cute for a daily-use household tool.

## Design Principles

1. **Optimize for "find it fast."** The core job is locating a recipe and reading it while cooking — minimize clicks and visual noise between opening the app and seeing ingredients/steps.
2. **Calm editorial structure over dashboard density.** Favor whitespace, clear typographic hierarchy, and restraint over cards-and-stats-everywhere.
3. **Respect shared ownership.** Household members add and edit collaboratively; public/private should read as an intentional sharing decision, not a buried toggle.
4. **One consistent interaction language.** A single confirmation pattern for destructive actions, one motion vocabulary, no novel pattern invented per page.
5. **Accessibility as baseline, not afterthought.** WCAG AA contrast and reduced-motion support apply everywhere by default.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA baseline contrast. Reduced-motion is respected globally (transform/animation durations collapse under `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`). No additional accommodation has been flagged by the household beyond these defaults.
